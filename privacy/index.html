Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly to us when you create an account, connect your Google Search Console property, or contact us. This includes your email address, name, and the Google OAuth token used to access your GSC data on your behalf.

When you connect Google Search Console, we read impression, click, and position data from properties you explicitly authorize. We do not access any other Google services or data beyond what is required to provide the TrackClusters service.

We also collect standard usage data (page views, feature interactions, error logs) to improve the product. This data is not linked to personally identifiable information beyond your account.

How We Use It

We use the information we collect to operate and improve TrackClusters: to sync your GSC keyword data, build and maintain your clusters, and display tracking reports.

We may use your email address to send you transactional emails (account notifications, billing receipts) and, with your consent, product updates and announcements. You can opt out of marketing emails at any time.

We do not sell your personal information or your GSC data to third parties. We do not use your GSC data to train machine learning models or for any purpose beyond providing the TrackClusters service to you.

Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on servers hosted in the United States (AWS). OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest. We use HTTPS for all data in transit.

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, we will delete your personal data and GSC data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

GDPR Rights

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • — Right of access — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • — Right to rectification — you may request correction of inaccurate data.
  • — Right to erasure — you may request deletion of your personal data.
  • — Right to data portability — you may request an export of your data in a machine-readable format.
  • — Right to object — you may object to processing of your data for marketing purposes.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the address below.

Cookies

This marketing site uses Google Analytics (GA4) to understand aggregate traffic. GA4 sets cookies to distinguish sessions. You can opt out via your browser's cookie settings or using the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to registered users by email. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision.

Third Parties

We use a limited number of third-party services to operate TrackClusters:

  • — Payment processing (Paddle) — Payment processing is handled by Paddle.com (Paddle.com Market Ltd), who acts as Merchant of Record for all transactions. Paddle processes your billing information, handles tax collection and compliance, and issues invoices on our behalf. Paddle is an independent data controller for payment data. Privacy policy: paddle.com/legal/privacy.
  • — Google Search Console API — We access your Google Search Console data on your behalf via Google's OAuth authentication. This data is used solely to provide the TrackClusters service and is never sold or shared with third parties. Google's data processing is governed by Google's Privacy Policy: policies.google.com/privacy.
  • — Google Analytics (GA4) — We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to collect anonymized usage data to improve the service. GA4 may use cookies. You can opt out via the cookie consent banner on this site or via Google's opt-out tools. Privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy.

We do not sell your personal information to any third party.

Contact

For privacy-related questions or to exercise your data rights, email us at hello@trackclusters.com .
